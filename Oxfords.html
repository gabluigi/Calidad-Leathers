Oxfords are considered the quintessential formal shoe, known for their sleek and polished look. They originated in Scotland and Ireland, but their name comes from Oxford University, where students popularized the style in the 1800s. Oxfords feature a closed lacing system, meaning the shoelace eyelets are stitched underneath the vamp, giving them a clean, streamlined appearance. They are the go-to option for business settings, formal events, and black-tie affairs. Typically made from high-quality leather, Oxfords come in various styles, including cap-toe, plain-toe, and wingtip, each offering slight variations in formality and design.
