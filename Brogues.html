Brogues have a rich history that traces back to the Irish and Scottish countryside. Initially designed as a practical outdoor shoe, the perforations (broguing) were meant to allow water to drain from the shoes when walking through wet terrain. Over time, brogues evolved into a stylish and semi-formal shoe, characterized by decorative perforations along the seams and toe cap. They can be worn in casual, semi-formal, and even business-casual environments. Brogues come in various styles, including full brogues (wingtips), semi-brogues, and quarter brogues, each offering a different level of decoration.
